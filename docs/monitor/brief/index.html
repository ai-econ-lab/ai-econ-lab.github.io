The pulse · Sweden in international context

Across the EU, firm AI adoption is climbing fast: the average reached 20% of enterprises in 2025, up from 8% in 2023 (Eurostat). Sweden is well above, at 35%, and its workforce ranks 2nd of 36 on generative-AI exposure. The series that moves every month is the Swedish depth cut below, the share of vacancies asking for an AI skill, 2006–2025, now about 2.08% (2025, provisional), roughly 140× its level twenty years ago.

International benchmark (AI Index / Lightcast, 2025): AI skills are required in a median 1.9% of postings across 22 countries; Sweden 2.8% on that measure. Not directly comparable to the Swedish JobTech series above (a narrower, live measure).

In focus · AI adoption by firm size in Sweden

Adoption climbs steeply with firm size, from 31% of small firms (10–49 employees) to 72% of large ones (250+) in 2025, every class up sharply since 2021.

Source: SCB, ICT usage in enterprises (NV0116), 2025. Full method at ai-econ-lab.github.io/monitor/#method.
