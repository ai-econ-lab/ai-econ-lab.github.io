About

An economics-led lab on AI and the future of work

The AI-Econ Lab studies how artificial intelligence is reshaping labour markets across countries, particularly for white-collar and service work, combining international comparisons with uncommonly broad access to linked register data: deepest in Sweden, reaching Denmark, Portugal and Germany, and expanding. Based at Örebro University and the Ratio Institute, we pair peer-reviewed research with open, citable public goods (the AIEL Monitor and the DAIOE exposure measure), so evidence on AI and work reaches policymakers, journalists and the public, not only specialists.

The lab

Who we are.

The AI-Econ Lab was initiated in 2019 and is led by associate professor Magnus Lodefalk in cooperation with professor Hildegunn Kyvik-Nordås. A steering group is tied to the lab (Lodefalk, Kyvik-Nordås, Schroeder and Görg).

The lab is located at Örebro University, a hub for AI development in Sweden, and is part of the university's Center for AI, Robotics and Cybersecurity (ARC). It has two partner institutions, the Ratio Institute and the Swedish Entrepreneurship Forum.

The lab is also a partner, and Örebro University a node, for the Swedish research cluster on AI, Structural Change and the Future of Work (AISCAF), funded by the Wallenberg AI, Autonomous Systems and Software Program – Humanity and Society (WASP-HS). AISCAF is co-led with Uppsala University and Stockholm University.

Data partners

Who shares data with us.

Several organisations give the lab access to data they collect themselves. None of them fund the lab, none of them see our results before publication, and listing them implies no endorsement. Where we publish figures from their data we say so on the figure, state what we did to it, and keep the underlying records private.

  • Akavia (site) · Member web panel on AI and working life, 2023 onwards. Shared with the lab; we publish aggregates with attribution.
  • Svea · Firm survey on AI adoption, run by Novus. Internal use only at present.
