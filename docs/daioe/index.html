Data-driven AI Occupational Exposure

DAIOE: how exposed is each job to AI?

DAIOE is the lab's measure of how exposed each occupation is to artificial intelligence, built from data rather than expert guesswork. It is openly published, versioned and mapped across the US (SOC), international (ISCO) and Swedish (SSYK) occupational classifications, so others can use it directly in their own work.

Download the data → See it applied in the Monitor

The measure

Data-driven, not expert-guessed.

DAIOE scores each occupation's exposure to AI from data, and publishes those scores openly and with versions, mapped across the US (SOC), international (ISCO) and Swedish (SSYK) classifications, so others can join it straight onto their own data.

Use it in your own work

Open data & crosswalks.

  • DAIOE datasets (all versions) · SOC / ISCO / SSYK, versioned
  • SSYK 2012 & SSYK 96 translation utility · crosswalk helper

Introduced and validated in the working paper “AI Unboxed and Jobs: A Novel Measure and Firm-Level Evidence from Three Countries”. See Research.
