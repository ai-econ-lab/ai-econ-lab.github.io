A multi-country, multi-disciplinary research lab

We study how artificial intelligence is reshaping the world of work.

An economics-led, multi-disciplinary research lab at Örebro University and RATIO, contributing to the WASP-HS research cluster AISCAF. We combine administrative registers from several European countries with job advertisements, surveys and public cross-country data. The AIEL Monitor is where part of that work becomes public: open indicators on AI and work across countries, with Sweden in uncommon depth, updated as the data arrive.

What the lab is

A research lab first.

The monitor is our measurement infrastructure made public: the same data and measures our own research runs on. We work across countries on public data and international comparisons, and, rarely for any lab, on linked employer–employee register data in several of them: deepest in Sweden, reaching Denmark, Portugal and Germany, and expanding to more. Economists work alongside sociologists, business scholars and computer scientists, because a labour market changed by AI cannot be read from one discipline or one country alone.

01 · DATA

Register-grade evidence

Linked employer–employee register data at population scale in Sweden: annual, with 4-digit occupations (LISA), and monthly individual employment (AGI); plus comparable access in a handful of other countries. Rare reach, paired with 10.9M public job ads.

02 · REACH

Multi-country

Register-level in Sweden, Denmark, Portugal and Germany, with more countries planned; 30-plus via EU-LFS and international job-ad data for external validity.

03 · LENS

Multi-disciplinary

Economics, sociology, business administration and computer science, partly through the WASP-HS cluster AISCAF, co-led with Uppsala and Stockholm.

04 · OUTPUT

Open public goods

Peer-reviewed research, plus citable, versioned public tools: the AIEL Monitor and the DAIOE exposure measure and Explorer.
